## The Motivation

The spring noise of topre keyboard can appear when the keyboard is used over time. You can choose to ignore the noise but it's still annoy sometime when you feel the different soundings of the keys, especially if you're a person who seek for perfection. 

## The Source of Spring Noise

According to a post on Reddit [1], the source of spring noise mainly comes from the strike when the spring bottom and the PCB. A graph illustration would be like this:

{{< image src="illustration.jpg" caption="Illustration of the Noise Source" width="50%">}}

There is also a video on YouTube reporting the same spring noise issue of Plum EC-S: [Plum 87 EC-S spring noise - Youtube](https://youtu.be/bYGL0GBpwd8), in the comments section it is mentioned that you can either lube your spring or stretch the spring a little bit. 

So in order to find the correct method to deal with the spring noise I've experienced several approaches on a single switch, and here is the result:

- **Lubing the guide rails**: :x:not working for the noise
- **Lubing the top of the spring**: :x:the noise remains
- **Stretching the spring a bit**: :x:not working either, and also **not recommended** cause you might mess up with the spring and end up with unnecessary problems (since the spring is the controller of the switch).
- **Lubing the bottom of the spring**: :heavy_check_mark:works perfectly well, the spring noise has disappeared after applying

So, that becomes more clear which method should be taken. In my case I don't have GP205 in hand so I used PMX-200 (silicon fluid, 1000cs) instead, and it worked pretty well.
